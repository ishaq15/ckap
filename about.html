About the Community Knowledge Archive Platform

Learn more about our vision, mission, and the impact we are creating across Nigeria and beyond.

Our Story

The Community Knowledge Archive Platform (CKAP) was developed by group 1 400L lis students as need to preserve Nigeria’s rich indigenous knowledge and make it accessible to students, researchers, and communities. As Library and Information Science students, we recognized the gap between traditional wisdom and modern digital tools. CKAP bridges that gap by digitizing oral histories, cultural practices, and local research outputs.

Our platform is not just about preservation — it is about empowerment. Communities earn recognition and income when their contributions are accessed, while students and researchers gain authentic resources for learning and innovation.

Quick Facts

  • Founded by: LIS students UniAbuja
  • Focus: Indigenous knowledge & digital literacy
  • Impact: Education, cultural preservation, community empowerment
  • Model: Social enterprise generating income

Our Vision

To be the leading digital hub for indigenous knowledge in Africa, connecting communities with global opportunities.

Our Mission

To preserve, organize, and share local knowledge using modern information tools, while creating sustainable income streams for contributors.

Our Values

Integrity, inclusivity, innovation, and respect for cultural heritage guide everything we do.
